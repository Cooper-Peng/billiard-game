How to Play Billiard

The objective in 8-ball pool is simple yet challenging: be the first player to legally pocket all of your designated balls (either stripes or solids) and then sink the 8-ball to win. The game begins with a break shot, where you'll aim to scatter the balls and ideally pocket one to continue your turn.

Use your mouse to aim the cue stick, adjusting both direction and power to execute the perfect shot. Advanced players can apply spin to the cue ball for more complex positioning and trick shots. Remember, strategy is just as important as skill – think several shots ahead and position the cue ball for your next move.

Master the fundamentals: control your power, plan your position, and always keep the 8-ball in mind for your final winning shot. With practice, you'll develop the skills to run the table like a pro!
